Fried Okra Recipe

Home

Description

Fried okra is a classic Southern side dish known for its crispy texture and savory flavor The recipe typically starts with fresh okra pods, which are sliced into bite-sized pieces. These are then coated in a seasoned cornmeal mixture—often with added flour, salt, pepper, and sometimes paprika or cayenne for a little heat.To ensure a good coating, the okra is usually dipped in buttermilk or beaten egg before dredging it in the dry mix. Once the pieces are fully coated, they’re deep-fried or pan-fried in hot oil until golden brown and crispy on the outside, while remaining tender on the inside.

The result is a flavorful and crunchy snack or side dish that pairs well with everything from fried chicken to barbecue Fried okra is beloved for its satisfying texture and ability to bring out the vegetable’s unique, slightly grassy flavor without being slimy, which can be a concern with other cooking methods. Served hot and often with a sprinkle of salt or a dipping sauce like ranch, this dish is a staple at many Southern tables and a great way to introduce newcomers to the charm of okra. Here's how to make this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 10 okra pods sliced into ¼-inch pieces.
  • 1 beaten egg
  • 1 cup Cornmeal
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup vegtable oil

Cooking Directions

  1. In a small Bowl, soak okra in egg for 5-10 minutes
  2. In a medium bowl, combine cornmeal, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Dredge okra in the cornmeal mixture, coating evenly.
  4. Carefully place okra in hot oil; stir continuously. Reduce heat to medium when okra first starts to brown, and cook until golden. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
